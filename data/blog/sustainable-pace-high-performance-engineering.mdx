Q2 pressure ramps: roadmaps tighten, incidents cluster, and “just one more sprint” becomes a lifestyle. **Sustainable pace** is not softness—it is how teams maintain defect rates and judgment over years.

## The burnout equation

```
Burnout ≈ High demands + Low control + Weak recovery + Isolation
```

Code harder does not appear in the formula. Recovery and agency do.

## What high performers protect

| Asset         | Protect with                                               |
| ------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------- |
| Sleep         | Consistent window; no hero deploys at 2am without rotation |
| Focus         | 2–3 hour deep blocks; Slack status honest                  |
| Judgment      | Fewer concurrent projects; WIP limits                      |
| Relationships | Pairing, mentorship, saying “I’m stuck” early              |

## Team practices that scale

- **No-meeting mornings** or focus Fridays
- **Incident retros without blame** — fixes process, not people
- **On-call compensation and rotation** — sustainability is operational
- **Definition of Done** includes tests and rollback plan

## Personal habits (realistic)

1. **Shutdown ritual** — write tomorrow’s first task; close laptop
2. **Movement** — 20-minute walk beats another espresso
3. **Boundaries** — one channel for urgent; everything else async
4. **Skill joy** — one learning block/week with no OKR attached

## When to escalate

If you dread Monday for months, or errors spike when you are tired, talk to your manager or HR. That is professionalism, not weakness.

## Reframe “productivity”

Measure:

- Defects escaped to prod
- Time to restore service
- Clarity of your last three PR descriptions

Not:

- Hours online
- Lines of code
- Busyness theater

## Closing

The engineers still shipping great work in 2036 are not the ones who won a marathon of crunch in 2026—they are the ones who **built systems and habits** that made excellence repeatable. Protect the long game.
